Machine Learning Based Diagnosis of Sleep-related Hypermotor Epilepsy 

Sleep-related hypermotor epilepsy (SHE) is often misdiagnosed as other sleep disorders with similar yet non-epileptic motor events, hindering patients' ability to receive proper treatment. Our research aimed to develop an effective machine learning model to diagnose SHE based on electroencephalogram data during NREM sleep. The accuracy of our model indicates the value of machine learning algorithms in differentiating SHE from healthy sleeping patterns, while the ease of use provides insight into the feasibility of application in the clinical setting.

Using Machine Learning Algorithms to Diagnose Sleep-related Hypermotor Epilepsy

Sleep-related hypermotor epilepsy (SHE) is a rare sleep disorder characterized by brief hypermotor seizures during the non-rapid eye movement (NREM) stages of sleep. An electroencephalogram (EEG) measures electrical activity from the brain by placing electrodes on specific parts of the scalp to record signals throughout sleep. Abnormalities in signal recordings during NREM stages can indicate a sleep disorder, and EEG data is commonly utilized when diagnosing SHE.

To develop our model, we employed 53 publicly available polysomnographic EEG recordings through random-forest, support-vector machine, and logistic regression learning algorithms. 70% of the EEG recordings were used to train while 30% was used to test. The model was trained on the extracted mean, minimum, maximum, standard deviation, and variance for the alpha, beta, theta, and delta waves of each subject's EEG recording.

Results indicate the model's ability to distinguish between EEG recordings of healthy patients and those with SHE. With a highest test accuracy of 81.3% in the random forest classifier, the model is not accurate enough to serve as an independent mode of SHE diagnosis. Howerver, it holds potential as an additional resource to assist the highly difficult SHE diagnosis process. Machine learning models of this type can support and expedite the manual process of EEG-based SHE diagnosis.

Bicycle Storage Optimization for 3440 North Lake Shore Drive 

HookUp, a tilt-based bicycle storage rack, reduces the physical strain involved in vertical bicycle storage while maximizing storage efficiency. Incorporating a mounted hook, upright stand, and wheel ramp, we designed HookUp to eliminate vertical lifting and require only the front half of a bicycle to be rotated. HookUp's freestanding structure provides vertical bicycle storage in areas previously not possible. Applied with our space optimization process, HookUp enables 10 additional bikes to be stored in 3440 N. Lake Shore Drive's recycling room alone.
